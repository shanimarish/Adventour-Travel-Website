Calle Crisologo

 Video Credits to Jepoy Travels


    Calle Crisologo, one of the most beautiful streets in the Philippines, is a 500-meter-long boulevard studded with Spanish-style homes built centuries ago. It is also a pedestrian-only district, further evoking the relaxed ambiance of the province's early days, where travelers can explore the street on foot or by horse-drawn carriages known as kalesas. A number of ancestral homes have been converted into eateries serving the renowned Ilocos bagnet (deep-fried pork belly) and empanadas. Alternatively, a handful of them have been converted into inns or souvenir shops selling handicrafts made by local artists. Frequently, these works of art are also displayed outside of the shops, making it convenient for tourists to window shop while taking in the scenery.

Puerto Princesa Underground River

 Video Credits to Alive Nature


    The Puerto Princesa Underground River is one of the New Seven Wonders of Nature and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The 8.2 kilometer-long river is renowned for its limestone cliffs, cave formations, and pure waters. While traveling on a paddleboat, tourists will be treated to breathtaking views here. After the visitors reach the caves, individuals will be surrounded by stalactites, stalagmites, birds, bats, and fish that are part of the ecosystem of the Puerto Princesa Underground River.

Banaue Rice Terraces

 Video Credits to Chai of the East


    The Batad Banaue Rice Terraces in Ifugao, Cordillera Region, are a man-made, stair-like rice paddy that follows the steep mountain slopes. The intricate irrigation system of the Batad Rice Terraces enables their continued use. A one-hour ascent to the village of Batad is required to witness the beauty of this ancient human ingenuity. Next, tourists will be greeted with panoramic views of pristine, untainted forest.

The Hundred Islands

 Video Credits to Benjoe Rivera Vidal


    The Hundred Islands are one of Luzon's most popular tourist destinations. There are more than 120 mushroom-like islands, each with unique characteristics. Here, the so-called Governor's Island, Quezon Island, and Children's Island are among the most frequented. There are white beaches, coral rock formations, beach forests, and a bat's cave for tourists to explore.

Mayon Volcano

 Video Credits to Oliver Collins Films


    Mayon Volcano, the fabled volcano with the perfect cone, is a 2,462-meter-high volcano that is visible from Legazpi City and other towns in Albay. In Mayon Volcano, you can get an even better view by riding an ATV through rocky and muddy terrain, climbing the lava wall, and taking in the breathtaking view as you stand close to the foot of the volcano.

Masungi Georeserve

 Video Credits to Rappler


    Masungi Georeserve has a strict policy of no reservations, no entry. This is an effort to regulate the amount of foot traffic within the preserve. Without jeopardizing safety, amazing rope sections and carefully placed pathways have been installed with minimal impact on the natural environment.
