Hinatuan Enchanted River

 Video Credits to Wonder World


    Hintuan Enchanted River The Enchanted River in Hinatuan, Surigao del Sur, is probably the bluest river in the country. It is a mystical gem nestled in the town of Hinatuan. It is 290 meters long, but its depth remains a mystery to many scuba divers, as there is no clear picture of what lies at the river's bottommost point. With water flowing to the Philippine Sea and the Pacific Ocean, the Enchanted Hinatuan River empties into an estuary where spring water and salt water mix.

Magpupungko Rock Pools

 Video Credits to Project Lupad


    Magpupungko Rock Pools features a breathtaking tidal rock pool with emerald waters that are crystal clear and surrounded by striking rock formations. This crown jewel is only visible during low tide and is inaccessible during high tide.

Mount Apo

 Video Credits to Yodi Insigne


    Mount Apo is the highest mountain in the Philippines and is located in Davao, the country's "Durian Capital." It rises to 2,956 meters above sea level and encompasses the provinces of Davao del Sur and North Cotabato. There are mossy forests, swamps, and volcanic structures on Mount Apo. Following the Kidapawan-Magpet Trail, hikers can traverse a variety of verdant landscapes in this region.

White Island

 Video Credits to The Eftimies


    White Island is a white-sand sandbar surrounded by clear blue waters just minutes from Camiguin's mainland. Due to its immaculate white sands and crystal clear blue waters, it is also known as the Naked Temptress. It is originally shaped like a boomerang, but its shape changes with the tides every year.

Philippine Eagle Centre

 Video Credits to Welcome To


    The Philippine Eagle Centre is an 8-hectare wildlife breeding center in Davao City that is home to the country's only captive eagles, the critically endangered Philippine Eagle. In addition to eagles, this wildlife refuge houses macaques and crocodiles. The Philippine Eagle Centre has educated its visitors for over two decades on the significance of preserving the haribon and its habitat.

Tinuy-an Falls

 Video Credits to One Armed Wanderer


    Tinuy-an Falls is a waterfall in Bislig, Surigao del Sur, that is approximately 95 meters wide and 60 meters deep. This three-tiered waterfall has natural pools that are ideal for swimming. The largest and tallest of the three layers is broad enough to accommodate bamboo rafts for visitors who wish to approach the waterfall drop. It is home to 91 species of animals, including amphibians, birds, reptiles, freshwater fish, and more. There are also 144 species of flora and 33 of which are nearing extinction.
