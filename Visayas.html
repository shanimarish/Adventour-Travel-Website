Visayas

Visayas are a group of about 6,000 islands in the middle of the country. They include the main islands of Cebu, Panay, Bohol Leyte, Negros and Samar. The Visayan people are known for their carefree and easy-going attitude.

White Beach Boracay

 Video Credits to Our Awesome Planet


    The White Beach Boracay is one of the most popular beaches in Visayas. It is 4 kilometers in length and has very fine white sand and clear blue water. White Beach is simply breathtaking and is the reason why Boracay has gained worldwide recognition. This beach is the primary tourist accommodation, dining, and entertainment area on Boracay. Additionally, this beach is lined with numerous Boracay restaurants, cafes, bars, and shops.

Chocolate Hills

 Video Credits to National Geographic


    The Chocolate Hills is one of Bohol's most popular attractions. This site is a popular tourist destination in Visayas and is frequently regarded as one of the world's natural wonders. Typically, green grass covers these hills. During the dry season, however, the grass turns brown, giving the hills their well-known chocolate brown hue. The hills vary in size, but from a distance they appear nearly symmetrical. This symmetry contributes to the site's extraordinary appearance.

Kawasan Falls

 Video Credits to Monskie Travels


    Kawasan Falls is the magnificent waterfall that attracts tens of thousands of tourists to Cebu Island. Kawasan attracts tourists from all over the world due to its untamed beauty and tranquil landscape. It is a great place to go swimming because the water is exceptionally clear and refreshing. The waterfall has two principal tiers with swimming-friendly natural pools. The first tier is approximately 40 meters tall. The second tier of a waterfall is typically less frequented because it requires a 10-minute hike to reach from the first cascade.

Moalboal Sardine Run

 Video Credits to Alex Chacon


    The Moalboal Sardine Run is one of the most spectacular underwater events in the Visayas region of Cebu, Philippines. This activity describes the experience of swimming through sardine schools. The sardine run is an entirely natural phenomenon! These sardines migrate from the deep end of the ocean to the shallow end in order to feed on the abundant planktons.

Islas de Gigantes

 Video Credits to The Juander Chaser


    Islas de Gigantes is a group of islands located off the coast of Estancia and Carles in Northeastern Iloilo. It is home to stunning lagoons, rock formations, caves, and beaches of white sand. The name of this island group means "Land of the Giants." This is due to the discovery of large human skeletons on these island and many people believed at the time that these skeletons belonged to giants.

Kalanggaman Island

 Video Credits to NowInPH


    The island of Kalanggaman is frequently referred to as "The Crown Jewel of Leyte." Due to its powdery white sand, stunning sandbars, and clear water, this island is one of the most popular Philippine island-hopping destinations. Visitors enjoy the tranquil, secure, and relaxing atmosphere of this location. It is not overdeveloped and rarely overpopulated. Visitors can relax under the palm trees and admire the beautiful Visayas scenery during their stay. On this island, visitors can engage in activities such as snorkeling, kayaking, and scuba diving if they wish to be more active.
